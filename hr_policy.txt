SECTION 1: PAID TIME OFF (PTO)

Full-time employees accrue PTO at the following rates:
- Years 0–2: 15 days per year (1.25 days/month)
- Years 3–5: 20 days per year (1.67 days/month)
- Years 6+:  25 days per year (2.08 days/month)

Part-time employees working 20+ hours per week accrue PTO at 50% of the full-time rate.
PTO must be approved by your direct manager at least 5 business days in advance for requests of 3 or more consecutive days.
Unused PTO of up to 5 days may be rolled over into the following calendar year. Anything above 5 days is forfeited on December 31st.
Employees may cash out up to 3 unused PTO days per year during the annual benefits window in November.

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY

Nexagen Biosciences supports a hybrid work model. Employees in eligible roles may work remotely up to 3 days per week.
All employees must be on-site on Tuesdays and Thursdays, which are designated collaboration days.
Employees must maintain a reliable internet connection of at least 25 Mbps and a dedicated, distraction-free workspace when working remotely.
Remote work privileges may be revoked by a manager with 2 weeks written notice if performance or collaboration standards are not met.
Employees working remotely are expected to be available and responsive during core hours: 9:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time.
New employees are required to work on-site full-time for their first 90 days before becoming eligible for hybrid work.

SECTION 3: HEALTH & BENEFITS

Nexagen offers medical, dental, and vision insurance through BlueCross BlueShield. Coverage begins on the first day of the month following your start date.
The company covers 80% of the premium for employee-only coverage and 60% for dependent coverage.
Employees may enroll in a Health Savings Account (HSA) with a company contribution of $500 per year for individual plans and $1,000 for family plans.
A 401(k) plan is available with a company match of up to 4% of salary. Employees are vested in company contributions after 3 years of service.
An Employee Assistance Program (EAP) is available to all employees and their immediate family members at no cost, providing up to 6 free counseling sessions per year.
Annual wellness stipend of $300 is available for gym memberships, fitness equipment, or mental health apps. Receipts must be submitted through the HR portal by December 15th.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT

All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and professionalism at all times.
Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination.
Employees must disclose any outside employment or consulting work to HR prior to starting such work. Conflicts of interest must be reported within 10 business days of becoming aware of them.
Company equipment, software, and data may not be used for personal business or shared with unauthorized parties.
All employees must complete annual compliance training by March 31st each year. Failure to complete training may result in suspension of system access.

SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: mid-year in July and annual in January.
Employees receive a rating on a 1–5 scale. Ratings of 3 or above are considered meeting expectations.
Merit increases are awarded annually in February based on performance ratings and budget availability. Employees rated 4 or 5 are eligible for increases of 3–6%. Employees rated 3 are eligible for increases of 1–3%.
Employees rated 1 or 2 for two consecutive review cycles may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
360-degree feedback from peers and direct reports is collected as part of the annual review process.

SECTION 6: PARENTAL LEAVE

Primary caregivers are eligible for 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child.
Secondary caregivers are eligible for 6 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Employees must have been employed for at least 12 months to be eligible for paid parental leave.
Leave must begin within 12 months of the birth or placement of the child.
Employees are guaranteed return to the same or equivalent role upon returning from parental leave.

SECTION 7: TERMINATION & OFFBOARDING

Employees wishing to resign are asked to provide a minimum of 2 weeks written notice.
Upon termination, all company equipment must be returned within 5 business days.
Final paychecks, including any accrued unused PTO (up to the rollover cap), will be issued within the timeframe required by applicable state law.
Access to all company systems will be revoked on the last day of employment.
Employees may be eligible for severance pay based on tenure and the circumstances of termination, as determined by HR and leadership on a case-by-case basis.
